Gupchup (20 points)

No. of Pools: 4
No. of teams per Pool: 2 (3 members each)
(At least 3 Y16 in total)
Rules:
  • There will be stipulated number of rounds, details of which will be disclosed on the spot.
  • The event will usually consist of one actor and two guessers.
  • The actor will act out movies/ places/ books/ personalities/ characters/ idioms/ movie
  • Dialogues/ songs the guessers have to guess the items in the minimum amount of time.
  • The rounds will be variations of the same principle.
Shabdrang (Word Games) (35 points)

No. of Pools: 4
Rules:
  • 1st Round:
    • Each pool will be given common word games exercises / puzzles / questions etc. along with the detailed instructions.
    • Specified time will be given to solve the booklet
    • Only one submission per pool will be accepted.
  • 2nd Round:
    • In round 2, each pool must bring 2 teams each consisting of 3 members (at least 3 Y16 in total)
    • Each pool will be introduced to a common live activity / competition at the venue itself.
  • Point Distribution
    • 1st Round - 40%
    • 2nd Round - 60%
Kathya (Audio Play) (30 points)

No. of Pools: 4
No. of submissions per Pool: 1
Rules:
  • Each pool will be shown five incomplete stories/themes. These incomplete parts will just be the seemingly dull foundations of an interesting story which can be built upon it.
  • The provided parts are merely to portray the central theme of the story around which the whole plot is to be woven by the participants. The pools will need to submit their audio plays.
  • Characters should be greater than or equal to 4 out of which at least [n/2], where [x/2] is the Greatest Integral Part of x, shall be Y15.
  • Time limit is 8-10 minutes, and breaking time constraints lead to penalty of 2% per 30 seconds exceeded.
  • Participants are free to create “play” out of any text, incident, or prose but completely copying from pre-existing piece will lead to cancellation.
  • Themes will be disclosed 7 days before the beginning of the event and allotted by lottery.
  • Participants are free to use music and other sounds in their play.

Judging Criteria:

Structure/Script 25
Theme 30
Voice modulation and Narration 30
Overall Impact 15

Nirvaak Case Study (30)

No. of Pools: 4
Rules:
  • Each pool will be provided a topic based on lottery, concerning some campus issue, regarding which campus junta needs to be informed. After getting a topic, they shall go on to collect facts and data regarding the same (this should not be a piece where an individual would express their view on the topic).
  • Then, a presentation of 8-10 minutes (three members, at least one Y15) should be prepared (MS-PPT/video presentation/ or as pool sees fit), regarding their findings, opinions and stands of all stake holders (if and as necessary) and finally, positive inputs and suggestions to improve the situation.
  • Presentation will be followed by Q/A round of 7 minutes.
  • Points distribution:
    • Presentation- 60 %
    • Report- 40%

NOTE
Presentation should reflect the work that has gone into investigation, so before presentation hardcopy and a soft copy of a report has also to be submitted, wherein all data collection has to be accounted for (survey reports/video showing people expressing themselves etc.). Participants must come to the competition venue with presentation (video/ppt, not necessary if entirely oral) and report. Though, report and presentation are judged separately, pools will lose marks if report is not deemed authentic.
